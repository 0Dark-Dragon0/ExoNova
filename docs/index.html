Project Overview

Mission Statement

The ExoNova project aims to develop a robust, scalable, and autonomous framework for Space Situational Awareness (SSA). By integrating state-of-the-art artificial intelligence with astrodynamics, we seek to mitigate the growing risks posed by space debris to critical orbital infrastructure.

Current tracking systems rely heavily on manual intervention and static threshold models. ExoNova introduces a dynamic, learning-based approach that adapts to the chaotic nature of orbital decay and non-cooperative objects.

Vision & Goals

  • Autonomous Tracking: Reduce latency between detection and cataloging of debris objects.
  • Predictive Accuracy: Achieve sub-meter prediction accuracy for LEO (Low Earth Orbit) conjunctions.
  • Collision Avoidance: Provide actionable Delta-V recommendations for autonomous maneuvering.
  • Orbital Safety: Contribute to global efforts to prevent the Kessler Syndrome.

The Orbital Debris Threat

The region of near-Earth space is becoming increasingly congested. Over 6,000 tons of debris are currently orbiting the Earth, traveling at velocities up to 17,500 mph. Even a fragment the size of a bolt carries kinetic energy capable of destroying a satellite.

Kessler Syndrome

A theoretical cascade scenario where collisions between objects generate debris that increases the likelihood of further collisions. Without intervention, specific orbits could become unusable for generations.

Economic & Scientific Loss

The cost of replacing damaged satellites is immense. Beyond economics, the loss of scientific instrumentation and communication infrastructure impacts global weather forecasting, navigation, and connectivity.

System Architecture

The ExoNova system follows a modular, layered architecture designed to ingest raw sensor data, process it through AI pipelines, and output actionable intelligence.

1. Sensor Layer

Aggregates data from ground-based radar, optical telescopes, and space-based surveillance (SBSS) networks. Raw data is time-synchronized and noise-filtered.

2. Data Processing Layer

Orbit determination algorithms (SGP4/SDP4) refine raw observations into state vectors. Data cleaning removes sensor artifacts and atmospheric interference.

3. AI & Prediction Layer

Deep learning models (LSTM/GRU) predict future state vectors accounting for non-gravitational perturbations like solar radiation pressure and atmospheric drag.

4. Decision & Visualization Layer

Reinforcement Learning agents evaluate collision probabilities and generate avoidance maneuvers. A real-time 3D dashboard visualizes conjunction events.

AI Models & Methodology

ExoNova utilizes a hybrid approach, combining classical astrodynamics with modern machine learning to overcome the limitations of purely physics-based models in high-density debris fields.

Time-Series Forecasting

LSTM Networks

Long Short-Term Memory networks are employed to capture long-term dependencies in orbital trajectories. They are exceptionally effective at modeling the decay of orbits affected by varying atmospheric density.

Sequence Processing

GRU Architecture

Gated Recurrent Units offer a more computationally efficient alternative to LSTMs for processing sensor streams. Used in the real-time tracking pipeline for rapid trajectory updates.

State Estimation

Kalman Filters

The Extended Kalman Filter (EKF) serves as the baseline for state estimation. It fuses noisy measurements from multiple sensors to produce the optimal estimate of the debris object's position and velocity.

Uncertainty Quantification

Monte Carlo Simulation

Used to propagate uncertainty through the orbit prediction. We run thousands of simulations with perturbed initial conditions to generate a probability cloud for collision events.

Decision Making

Reinforcement Learning

An RL agent (PPO algorithm) is trained to optimize Delta-V maneuvers. It learns to balance the fuel cost of maneuvering against the probability of collision, maximizing satellite lifespan.

Pattern Recognition

CNN for Optical Data

Convolutional Neural Networks assist in the classification of optical telescope images, distinguishing between debris, stars, and active satellites to reduce false positives.

Future Scope & Roadmap

While Phase 1 focuses on tracking and prediction, the roadmap for ExoNova extends into active debris remediation and deep space applications.

  • Real-Time Satellite Integration Establish direct telemetry uplinks to operational satellites to close the loop between prediction and maneuver execution without human ground control.
  • Autonomous Maneuver Execution Upgrade the RL agents to have direct control over propulsion systems, enabling fully autonomous "evasive maneuvers" in critical emergencies.
  • Global Sensor Networks Integrate data from private sector radar arrays and university observatories to create a unified, global observation mesh for improved coverage.
  • Space Debris Removal Planning Extend the AI models to optimize capture trajectories for active debris removal (ADR) missions, such as harpoons or nets.
  • Deep Space Extension Adapt the tracking algorithms for cis-lunar space and Mars transfer orbits to support future interplanetary exploration missions.
