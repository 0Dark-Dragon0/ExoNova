The Orbital Threat

The region of near-Earth space is becoming increasingly congested. Over 6,000 tons of debris are currently orbiting the Earth, traveling at velocities up to 17,500 mph. Even a fragment the size of a bolt carries kinetic energy capable of destroying a satellite.

Kessler Syndrome

A theoretical cascade scenario where collisions between objects generate debris that increases the likelihood of further collisions. Without intervention, specific orbits could become unusable for generations.

Economic Impact

The cost of replacing damaged satellites is immense. Loss of scientific instrumentation and communication infrastructure impacts global weather forecasting, navigation, and connectivity.

System Architecture

1

Sensor & Data Layer

Aggregates data from ground-based radar, optical telescopes, and space-based surveillance (SBSS) networks. Raw data is time-synchronized using GPS timestamps and noise-filtered to remove atmospheric artifacts.

2

Orbit Determination (SGP4)

Propagates Two-Line Element (TLE) sets. Uses Simplified General Perturbations (SGP4) algorithms to refine raw observations into State Vectors (Position & Velocity) accounting for Earth's oblateness (J2 effect).

3

AI Prediction Engine

Deep Learning models (LSTM/GRU) predict future state vectors accounting for non-gravitational perturbations like Solar Radiation Pressure (SRP) and variable atmospheric dragâ€”factors physics models often miss.

AI Models & Methodology

ExoNova utilizes a hybrid approach, combining classical astrodynamics with modern machine learning to overcome the limitations of purely physics-based models in high-density debris fields.

TIME-SERIES

LSTM Networks

Long Short-Term Memory networks are employed to capture long-term dependencies in orbital trajectories. Exceptional at modeling decay affected by atmospheric density variances.

  • > Captures long-term temporal dependencies
  • > Robust against noise in TLE data
SEQUENCE PROCESSING

GRU Architecture

Gated Recurrent Units offer a more computationally efficient alternative to LSTMs. Used in the real-time tracking pipeline for rapid trajectory updates.

  • > Faster convergence than LSTM
  • > Lower computational overhead
STATE ESTIMATION

Extended Kalman Filter

The EKF serves as the baseline for state estimation. It fuses noisy measurements from multiple sensors to produce the optimal estimate of position and velocity.

  • > Non-linear system handling
  • > Optimal state vector fusion
UNCERTAINTY

Monte Carlo Simulation

Used to propagate uncertainty through orbit prediction. We run thousands of simulations with perturbed initial conditions to generate a probability cloud.

  • > Probabilistic risk assessment
  • > Collision Probability (Pc) calculation
DECISION MAKING

Reinforcement Learning

An RL agent (PPO algorithm) is trained to optimize Delta-V maneuvers. It learns to balance fuel cost against collision probability.

  • > Autonomous maneuver planning
  • > Maximizes satellite lifespan
PATTERN RECOGNITION

CNN for Optical Data

Convolutional Neural Networks assist in classifying optical telescope images, distinguishing debris from stars and active satellites.

  • > Image classification
  • > Reduces false positives

Future Scope & Roadmap

Real-Time Satellite Integration

Establish direct telemetry uplinks to operational satellites to close the loop between prediction and maneuver execution without human ground control.

Autonomous Maneuver Execution

Upgrade the RL agents to have direct control over propulsion systems, enabling fully autonomous "evasive maneuvers" in critical emergencies.

Active Debris Removal (ADR)

Extend the AI models to optimize capture trajectories for removal missions, such as harpoons or nets, actively cleaning the orbital shell.

Deep Space Extension

Adapt the tracking algorithms for cis-lunar space and Mars transfer orbits to support future interplanetary exploration missions.
