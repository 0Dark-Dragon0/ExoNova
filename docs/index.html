Project Overview

ExoNova is an advanced research-oriented system designed to track, predict, and mitigate collision risks caused by space debris. The system combines classical orbital mechanics with modern artificial intelligence techniques to create a scalable and future-ready space situational awareness framework.

Unlike traditional tracking systems that rely purely on deterministic models, ExoNova integrates probabilistic AI predictions, uncertainty estimation, and continuous learning from historical orbital data.

System Architecture

The ExoNova architecture is divided into four logical layers:

  • Data Acquisition Layer (Radar, Optical, TLE)
  • Physics-Based Propagation Layer
  • AI Prediction & Risk Scoring Layer
  • Decision & Visualization Layer

Each layer operates independently yet shares standardized interfaces, allowing modular upgrades and research experimentation.

AI & Prediction Logic

ExoNova employs machine learning models such as:

  • LSTM / GRU for trajectory prediction
  • Kalman Filters for noise reduction
  • Monte Carlo simulations for uncertainty
  • Reinforcement Learning for avoidance planning

The AI models continuously learn from updated orbital data, solar activity variations, and historical conjunction events.
